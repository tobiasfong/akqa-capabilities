The Opportunity

AKQA builds the digital banks that people actually use every day.

Financial services across Asia Pacific are shifting to digital. From Malaysia to Vietnam to the Philippines, customers want to open accounts, make payments, and manage their money without visiting a branch. In New Zealand, a market where 61% of life insurance customers are underinsured, digital tools are changing how advisers reach and serve clients. In Australia, In1Bank is building retail banking from the ground up, natively digital. The institutions that get this right grow faster. The ones that don't lose customers to those that do.

AKQA works with banks and financial services companies to design and build these digital experiences — from the first screen a customer sees to the tools they use daily. That includes onboarding flows, personalised financial guidance, payment interfaces, interactive advice tools, and the design systems that keep everything consistent across markets and products.

AKQA's approach starts with research. AKQA studies how people actually bank and make financial decisions, runs design thinking workshops with client teams, then builds and tests iteratively. The result is digital products that work well, look right for each market, and keep customers coming back — as demonstrated by 82% quarterly active users at Techcombank and 64% of Partners Life NZ customers completing financial fact-finding independently.

What We Do
01
Digital Banking Platforms
AKQA designs and build complete digital banking products — from concept through to launch. For UOB, this covered neo banking, retail, wealth, SME, and corporate banking, all unified under a single design system shared across Southeast Asian markets.
02
Mobile Onboarding
AKQA designs account opening flows that work entirely on mobile — replacing branch visits with guided digital journeys. Techcombank onboarded one million users and businesses through this approach.
03
Personalised Financial Tools
AKQA builds recommendation engines and personal finance tools that give customers relevant guidance — like the AI assistant Aqil for Bank Islam's Be U, or the wealth planner in UOB TMRW that helps customers set and track investment goals.
04
Payments & Daily Banking
AKQA designs payment experiences people enjoy using. For Sacombank in Vietnam, that meant character-driven interfaces and emotional design that turned a utility into something customers actively engage with.
05
Design Systems Across Markets
AKQA creates shared design systems that keep a bank's digital products consistent across countries and teams, while allowing each market to adapt for local needs. This is how banks like UOB and Standard Chartered scale without losing quality.

Case Studies
UOB

A decade of digital banking transformation across ASEAN

Over ten years, AKQA has designed and developed more than fifty products for UOB — from UOB TMRW, which unified the Mighty and TMRW apps across Southeast Asia, to SME banking powered by UOB Infinity. The partnership has digitally enabled 5.3 million customers across the region.
ING

Building the first digital bank in the Philippines

AKQA worked with ING to reimagine the banking experience in the Philippines, overcoming a traditionally conservative industry. The team adapted and contributed to the global design system, developed branding and UX guidelines, and scaled the team from a single lead to a full local operation.
Partners Life

Making life insurance advice interactive and independent

New Zealand is one of the most underinsured developed nations. AKQA built Evince for Partners Life — a visual, interactive advice tool that helps customers understand their life insurance needs based on personal risk. The tool cut the advice process from 23 days to 11, and 64% of customers now complete fact-finding independently without an adviser.
