The Opportunity

Every journey begins before departure. The experience should start there too.

Travel and hospitality brands compete on experience — but the digital touchpoint is often the weakest link. From booking to check-in to post-stay engagement, guests and travellers expect the same attention to detail online that they receive in person. The gap between physical excellence and digital mediocrity is where loyalty is won or lost.

Intelligent platforms close that gap. Multi-brand hotel ecosystems consolidate fragmented booking experiences into a single, intuitive journey. Connected wildlife platforms transform attraction visits into immersive, educational adventures. City-wide sporting campaigns turn events into cultural moments that define destinations for global audiences.

AKQA builds the digital experience layer for travel and hospitality across Asia-Pacific. In Thailand, AKQA partnered with Centara to relaunch their multi-brand hotel platform. In Singapore, AKQA built the connected wildlife experience for Mandai — serving 1,000+ species and 15,000 animals. In Australia, AKQA works with Tourism Victoria. In New Zealand, Tātaki Auckland Unlimited. In Japan, AKQA created the "Runners Helping Runners" campaign for the Tokyo Marathon — mobilising 66 million media impressions and achieving the highest Nike footwear market share ever recorded at the race. Customer journey mapping, user personas, and personalised booking flows form the foundation across the region.

What We Do
01
Hotel Website Redesign — Thailand
For Centara, we rebuilt their multi-brand hotel website through customer journey mapping and user research. AKQA designed simplified booking flows, personalised rate pages, and destination guides that increased conversion and guest satisfaction across their portfolio.
02
Connected Wildlife Experience — Singapore
For Mandai Wildlife Group in Singapore, AKQA built a connected experience platform serving 1,000+ species and 15,000 animals across multiple parks. The platform transforms attraction visits into immersive, educational journeys — connecting conservation storytelling with digital wayfinding and visitor engagement.
03
Tourism Platform — Australia
For Visit Victoria (Tourism Victoria), AKQA delivers digital platforms that promote one of Australia's most-visited states to domestic and international travellers. The work spans destination marketing, content strategy, and digital experiences that turn inspiration into bookings.
04
Destination Marketing — New Zealand
For Tātaki Auckland Unlimited, AKQA builds digital experiences that position Auckland as a world-class destination. The platform supports major events, cultural programming, and visitor engagement — connecting New Zealand's largest city with travellers across APAC and beyond.
05
City-Wide Sporting Campaign — Japan
For Nike at the Tokyo Marathon, AKQA created "Runners Helping Runners" — a campaign that made running a city-wide movement. Olympian Suguru Osako served as Commander; comedian Yuriyan Retriever as Chief Cheering Officer. The campaign achieved the highest Nike footwear market share ever recorded at the race and won the People's Voice award.

Case Studies
Mandai

A connected wildlife experience for Singapore's world-class parks

AKQA built a connected experience platform for Mandai Wildlife Group — serving 1,000+ species and 15,000 animals across Singapore Zoo, Night Safari, River Wonders, and Bird Paradise. The platform transforms attraction visits into immersive, educational journeys through digital wayfinding, conservation storytelling, and visitor engagement.
Centara

Digitalising world-class Thai hospitality across global markets

AKQA worked with Centara to build an intuitive, multi-brand website relaunch. Customer journey mapping workshops, user personas, and extensive research identified key targets. The team enhanced experiences through personalised membership rates, destination recommendations, and a satisfying booking journey for end users.
Nike

Runners Helping Runners: mobilising Tokyo for the marathon

AKQA created a campaign around the Tokyo Marathon that made running a city-wide movement. Olympian Suguru Osako served as Commander; comedian Yuriyan Retriever as Chief Cheering Officer. The campaign ran across FAQ short films, podcasts, in-store, and the Nike app — achieving the highest Nike footwear market share ever recorded at the Tokyo Marathon and winning the People's Voice award in the Sports category.
