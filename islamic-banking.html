The Opportunity

Islamic finance is growing fast in Southeast Asia. The digital experience needs to keep up.

Across Southeast Asia and beyond, a new generation of customers wants Shariah-compliant banking and investment products they can use on their phones. Islamic finance is no longer confined to its traditional centres — digital platforms now reach Muslim communities across the wider APAC region, including diaspora populations who have historically had limited access to compliant financial products. The challenge is building platforms that are as principled as the finance they serve, while being as convenient as anything else on the market.

For Bank Islam in Malaysia, we designed Be U — the country's first cloud-native Islamic digital bank, built to serve university students and young adults. It includes a personal AI assistant called Aqil (meaning 'wise' in Arabic), personal finance management tools, and a gig marketplace for income opportunities. For BSI in Indonesia, we built a mobile app with adaptive dashboards and personalised financial stories. For MIDF, a Shariah-compliant stock and ETF trading platform.

Each project started with research — field studies, empathy mapping, and usability testing — to understand what customers in each market actually need. The branding, design systems, and product features all followed from there.

Case Studies

AKQA conceptualised and designed Be U — Bank Islam's first digital banking product targeting a younger generation.
