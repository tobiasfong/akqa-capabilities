The Opportunity

Every citizen touchpoint is a service. Every service is the nation.

Across the Asia-Pacific, public sector organisations manage complex ecosystems spanning defence logistics, enterprise development, housing, emergency response, education, and social services. From Singapore's government agencies to New Zealand's national coastguard and Australia's leading universities, citizens and workers interact with dozens of disconnected platforms, each with its own logic. The opportunity is to unify these touchpoints into intelligent, people-centred experiences where the service navigates to the citizen, not the other way around.

AI-powered platforms replace static portals with predictive safety systems, immersive engagement tools, and connected workspaces. In New Zealand, machine learning analyses live sea conditions to protect boat users at scale. In Australia, virtual open days set new records for accessibility and attendance. In Singapore, enterprise support becomes self-service and housing journeys start from user insight. Across the region, the interface shifts from navigating government structure to government understanding and anticipating the needs of the people it serves.

AKQA builds the experience layer that connects public institutions to the people they serve across Singapore, New Zealand, and Australia. Design systems create consistency across agencies. AI and data platforms enable real-time decision-making in critical services. Multilingual, accessible, and privacy-first by design, every platform is built sovereign and secure — ready for the scale and sensitivity that public service demands across the APAC region.

What We Do
01
Connected Citizen Services
HDB built a digital ecosystem that guides home buyers and owners through property journeys with transparency and clarity. The platform connects citizens to information, to services, and to the nation's housing mission — intelligent, accessible, and designed for life at every stage.
02
Enterprise Growth Ecosystems
Enterprise Singapore partnered with AKQA to serve 290,000 businesses through an integrated website and MarTech ecosystem. The platform reduced friction for business owners, enabled self-service support, and created data sharing between departments — making it easier for enterprises to access the guidance they need to grow.
03
Transport Management Systems
MINDEF needed a unified platform to manage vehicle acquisition, assignment, and tracking across military operations. AKQA partnered with ST Engineering and SMRT to design a system that replaced manual processes with mobile-optimised coordination, giving soldiers and operations staff transparency and efficiency in every indent.
04
AI-Powered Safety Infrastructure
Coastguard New Zealand partnered with AKQA to build a predictive search and rescue system that analyses live boat journeys, weather, swell, and location data in real time. Machine learning surfaces risk signals before incidents occur — protecting up to 750,000 boat users across New Zealand's coastline and reaching over 10,000 app downloads in its initial period.
05
Immersive Public Education Platforms
Deakin University in Melbourne partnered with AKQA to reimagine its open day as a fully digital experience. The platform delivered live Q&As, one-to-one sessions, virtual campus tours, and a gamification layer rewarding exploration — setting a new record for open day attendance and running for two consecutive years as an ongoing engagement tool for students across Australia and abroad.

Case Studies
Mandai

Martech for wildlife and conservation

Wildlife Reserves Singapore collaborated with Aleph to deliver a cross-platform experience for Mandai. The work centred on a personable, easy-to-use, scalable e-commerce ecosystem for the Mandai and conservation community, supported by research, UX/UI, content, and MarTech reporting.
Enterprise Singapore

Inspiring businesses to grow beyond their current capabilities

AKQA built an integrated Martech ecosystem and redesigned Enterprise Singapore's website to reduce friction for business owners seeking support. The platform enabled internal data sharing between departments and delivered self-service tools that made it easier for the agency to guide enterprises on their growth journey.
Inland Revenue

Digital experience platform for public-service transformation

Inland Revenue's Business Transformation Programme set out to improve customer experience, support digital channel shift, and reduce cost to serve. The web content management and digital experience platform work, awarded to AKQA and Sitecore, supported ongoing customer-experience initiatives including the rebuild of ird.govt.nz.
